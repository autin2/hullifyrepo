Types of Boats — Quick Uses, Photos & Tips

Shopping or just learning the lingo? Here’s a plain-English tour of common boat styles and when to choose each. Drop your photos right into the image slots—filenames are listed under every card.

Catamaran

StableFamilyOvernightSailing
  • Two hulls = wide deck space and low heel angle; great for kids and comfort.
  • Shallow draft for beaches/sandbars; big saloon on larger cats.
  • Downside: more beam (slips & haul-outs cost more).
Learn sailing basics

Center Console

FishingAll-aroundSaltwater
  • Walk-around fishability with high freeboard and self-bailing deck.
  • Single or twin outboards for speed and range; easy wash-down.
  • Families like the open deck; add cushions and shade to boost comfort.
Selling tips

Sportfisher (Offshore)

OffshoreFishingDieselCabin
  • Deep-V hulls, long range, towers, outriggers—built to chase pelagics.
  • Comfortable cabins for overnights; higher fuel/maintenance costs.

Bowrider

FamilyLakesDay boating
  • Open bow seating = social layout; easy trailer & storage sizes.
  • Good for tubing and coves; not ideal for big offshore days.

Pontoon

EntertainingCalm waterHuge seating
  • Massive deck space; simple operation and low draft.
  • Tri-toons add speed and rough-water capability.
