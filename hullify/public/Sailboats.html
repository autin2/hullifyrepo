Sailboats 101 — Sailing Basics, Trim, Tacks & Gybes

A calm intro to sailing: how the wind moves your boat, what each line does, and exactly how to tack, gybe, reef, and stop the boat safely.

1) Rig Basics: Parts & Lines

Standing rigging holds the mast up (shrouds, stays). Running rigging moves sails (halyards raise, sheets trim, vang/kicker controls boom downforce, traveler moves boom sideways, outhaul & cunningham/halyard control sail shape).

Common rigs: sloop (most popular), cat rig (single sail), ketch/yawl (two masts). Learn your boat’s reef points and traveler position.

2) Points of Sail & the No-Go Zone

  • No-go zone: ~35–45° either side of the wind; sails luff.
  • Close-hauled → Close reach → Beam reach → Broad reach → Run.
  • Turn toward the wind to slow (luff), away from the wind to power up.

Watch ripples and flags. Keep apparent wind in mind as speed builds.

3) Sail Trim with Telltales

  • Rule: Trim in until luffing just stops; if the inside telltale lifts, trim a touch; if the outside flutters, ease a touch.
  • Power vs control: Traveler for angle, sheet for twist; vang flattens leech downwind; outhaul/cunningham flatten in breeze.
  • Heel control: Ease sheets or traveler, reef early, and steer up in gusts.

4) How to Tack (Step-by-Step)

  1. Call: “Ready about?” Crew replies: “Ready.”
  2. Helm: push tiller to leeward (or turn wheel into the wind); boat passes head-to-wind.
  3. Jib: release working sheet as sail luffs; trim the new sheet smoothly as it fills.
  4. Main: centerline through the wind, then ease to the new close-hauled angle.
  5. Steer a few seconds to build speed before trimming tight.

5) How to Gybe (Safe & Controlled)

  1. Call: “Stand by to gybe.” Bring the boat onto a deep reach; control the boom (sheet in slightly).
  2. Turn gently through the wind from astern; as the main crosses, ease sheet out under control.
  3. Use a preventer on longer downwind legs to avoid accidental gybes (but release before turns).

Keep crew low and clear of the boom arc.

6) Reef Early + Heave-To

  • Reef before you need to: add a reef when heel >20° or handling feels sketchy.
  • Heave-to: tack without releasing the jib, center/secure the tiller to windward, ease main—boat settles calmly for breaks or heavy weather.

7) Right-of-Way Essentials

  • Power generally gives way to sail, but not if sailboat is overtaking or restricted vessels are involved.
  • Port vs starboard tack: Starboard-tack boat stands on; port-tack keeps clear.
  • Leeward over windward on same tack; overtaking boat keeps clear.
  • Maintain a lookout and avoid collisions regardless of “rights.”

8) Pre-Sail Checklist

  • PFDs for all; kill switch & first-aid aboard; VHF on Ch. 16.
  • Rig check: halyards run free, shackles secure, battens/slug in, reef lines led.
  • Forecast & tide reviewed; float plan filed; nav lights functional.
  • Practice tack, gybe, and heave-to in open water before crowded areas.

Motoring in/out of marinas is standard—drop sails outside the fairway.
